# CATIA Stanley Cup 3D Model
A parametric 3D model of the Stanley Cup built in CATIA V5 as part of EGR 120 — Introduction to Engineering Design at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University. What looked straightforward turned into a solid exercise in surface modelling, part design, and assembly — particularly around the lid fixture and rubber seal components.

## Overview
The model was built from scratch in CATIA V5, starting with 2D sketches and working up to a fully assembled, coloured, and material-assigned 3D model. The project covered the full part-to-assembly workflow and introduced the challenges of modelling curved and irregular geometry.

## What Was Built
The Stanley Cup was modelled as a multi-part assembly including:
- Main body (cup and outer shell)
- Lid fixture — the most geometrically complex component, requiring careful surface modelling
- Rubber seal/fixture — irregular geometry that didn't lend itself to simple extrusions
- Handle and structural components
- Full assembly with colour and material assignments applied per part

## Tools & Features Used
- **CATIA V5** — primary modelling environment
- **Part Design** — solid feature creation (pads, pockets, fillets, chamfers)
- **Sketcher** — 2D profile creation as the base for all features
- **Surface Modelling** — used for the more complex curved geometry on the lid and rubber components
- **Assembly Design** — parts assembled with constraints
- **Material & Appearance** — colour and material properties applied to the final assembly
